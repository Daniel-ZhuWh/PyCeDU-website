An adaptive Python learning platform for every student, every classroom.

Our product is an adaptive online learning tool that aids teachers and students in learning and teaching programming more effectively in-class.

Design Brief

We are a team of five master students from Carnegie Mellon University partnered with Cross Domain to envision a product that supports programming learning in Chinese middle schools. We embarked on a strongly user-driven end-to-end research, design, and development process which has culminated in the creation of Cross Domain Collaborate.

With the promising development of K-12 programming education in China these days, Cross Domain decided to embrace this new opportunity and expand its market. As the first step, the company plans to design practical learning tools that support Python elective courses in middle schools. In particular, its targets include public and experimental schools in first-tier cities.

By researching K-12 programming standards and existing online Python courses, our team will apply learning science principles in the design of a supportive Python learning tool for Chinese middle school students at international and experimental schools. We aim to boost our learners’ motivation with better student-teacher interaction and emphasize their sense of achievement by incorporating more adaptive learning technologies.
